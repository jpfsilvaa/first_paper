- Overtime has an adverse effect on the hospital, the physicians, and the patients. From a profit perspective, overtime could result in a financial loss for hospitals because overtime staffing costs are expensive [1,15,33] . From an efficiency viewpoint, overtime leads to stress and dissatisfaction in physicians and patients, further de- creasing the surgical efficiency. Sometimes the scheduled surgical work is even cancelled due to excessive overtime [7,43] . By and large, overtime is inevitable without dramatically underutilizing the ORs but can be mitigated by effective scheduling. Hence, man- agers continue to seek methodologies for mitigating overtime in surgical scheduling.
- To mitigate overtime, the related literature can typically be categorized into two classes: the first controls the probability of overtime and the second minimizes the magnitude of overtime
